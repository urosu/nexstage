# Mailchimp

**URL:** https://mailchimp.com
**Target customer:** Very broad — originally SMB generalist email, now re-positioning as ecommerce marketing platform after Intuit acquisition (2021). Primary base: small-to-medium non-ecommerce businesses, solopreneurs, newsletter publishers, agencies. Ecommerce push is newer (2022–2026) and positioned against Klaviyo/Omnisend. 12M+ users claimed; ~60% are freemium or Essentials-tier non-ecommerce. Feb 2026 "omnichannel dashboard" launch is their most aggressive ecommerce play to date.
**Pricing:** Four tiers + free, billed by contact count. Customer Journey Builder and most ecommerce automation require Standard ($20+/mo). Historically prices climb aggressively with list size, and Mailchimp charges for unsubscribed contacts unless archived.
- **Free** — $0. 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo, Mailchimp branding on every email, no scheduling, no multi-step automation, no A/B, no segmentation beyond basic. Reduced from 500 contacts (2023) and 2,000 (2022).
- **Essentials** — from $13/mo (500 contacts, 5,000 sends/mo). 5k contacts = $75/mo. Basic automation (single-step autoresponders), A/B testing, 3 audiences max, 500k monthly send cap.
- **Standard** — from $20/mo (500 contacts). 5k contacts = $100/mo. 100k contact cap, 12× send-limit multiplier. **Customer Journey Builder unlocks here** — required for cart abandonment, welcome series, post-purchase.
- **Premium** — from $350/mo (up to 10k contacts). Unlimited audiences, priority support, multivariate testing, advanced segmentation.
- **Mailchimp for Shopify / WooCommerce** — free app connecting a store; does not change pricing tier.
**Positioning one-liner:** Pre-2026 — "The all-in-one marketing platform for small businesses." Post-Feb-2026 rebrand — "Profitable ecommerce marketing, powered by Intuit data." A generalist tool pivoting hard toward ecommerce with Mailchimp Site Tracking Pixel + omnichannel dashboard + Intuit QuickBooks data.

## What they do well
- **Reports hub is a top-level nav item, not buried.** Click Reports → see email campaigns, SMS, landing pages, social posts, popup forms, automations, comparative reports, all sub-tabbed. E-commerce sub-tab appears when a store is connected.
- **Comparative Reports (Premium)** allows side-by-side campaign metrics across multiple campaigns — purpose-built screen for answering "which campaign performed best." Mailchimp owns this pattern in the category.
- **Industry benchmarks** drawn from 481M historical campaigns across 41 industries — largest dataset in the category. Embedded on individual campaign reports as "your open rate vs industry average."
- **E-commerce Dashboard (post-Feb 2026)** unifies email, SMS, automation performance, and ecommerce events in one view. Revenue attributed to Mailchimp, top products sold via email, CLV tile.
- **Mailchimp Site Tracking Pixel (Feb 2026)** — their own first-party pixel, pitched as iOS-14 workaround and attribution lift. Mirrors Klaviyo Active on Site + Triple Pixel patterns.
- **Customer Journey reports track funnel completion per automation.** Per-step metrics on each journey card, similar to Klaviyo flow analytics.
- **Click maps in every email report** — heatmap visualization of where each recipient clicked. Reliable, standard feature.
- **Bot filtering on opens/clicks** — acknowledges MPP and bot inflation, lets users toggle filtering on campaign reports.
- **Google Analytics integration built-in** for landing pages + campaigns. Meta Pixel for conversion tracking. Cleaner handoff to external BI than Omnisend.
- **Revenue Attribution report** tells users which emails/texts drove which sales with a single figure: total revenue attributed, avg revenue per recipient, avg order revenue.
- **Product activity view** on every email report: list of all SKUs sold through that specific campaign, with revenue totals per product.
- **Predictive analytics introduced in 2025–2026:** forecasts when audiences are most likely to engage or convert (send-time optimization). Not as deep as Klaviyo's CLV/churn but present.
- **Multi-audience support (Standard 3 audiences, Premium unlimited)** — useful for agencies managing many clients under one login.
- **SMS expanding globally in 2026** — historically US/UK only; now in wider rollout.
- **AI Marketing Assistant** (2025+) recommends send times, subject lines, segmentation moves.

## Weaknesses / common complaints
- **Ecommerce features are "mediocre compared to platforms built for ecommerce."** Product recommendations are basic, segmentation lacks depth (vs Klaviyo), revenue attribution less granular. Every serious ecommerce brand eventually migrates off.
- **Pricing went up, free tier shrunk, every year since Intuit acquired.** Free plan: 2,000 contacts (2022) → 500 (2023) → 250 (2026). Users perceive Mailchimp as becoming steadily less generous.
- **Unsubscribed contacts count toward your billable total** unless manually archived. Users routinely overpay because of passive list bloat.
- **Customer Journey Builder requires Standard ($20/mo minimum)** — abandoned cart, welcome series, post-purchase flows are gated. Free and Essentials users get only single-step autoresponders. This is the hardest paywall in the category for basic ecommerce use cases.
- **Shopify integration syncs break.** Recurring review theme on Shopify App Store: "App & store not syncing properly, despite manual resync." Support response times often exceed 48 hours.
- **Customer support is widely considered declined-quality.** "Live expert help" advertising is misleading; first-level support gives generic troubleshooting.
- **Automation branching logic is less flexible than Klaviyo or Omnisend.** Ecommerce-specific triggers limited. Cart-abandonment timing cannot be fine-tuned without workarounds.
- **Segmentation depth weaker than Klaviyo.** Predicted CLV as a segment filter is newer + less robust.
- **Reporting UI feels dated** — Mailchimp's reports have a 2015-era aesthetic; Klaviyo and Omnisend both feel more modern.
- **Deliverability is fine but not industry-leading.** Large shared-IP infrastructure means deliverability can spike or sag based on other senders' behavior.
- **Intuit ownership changes tone.** QuickBooks data integration and upsell to Intuit ecosystem is visible inside the product; users who came for email marketing resent the cross-sell.
- **Shopify App Store rating is lower than Omnisend/Klaviyo** — 3.5–4.0 range vs Omnisend's 4.7. Recent reviews skew negative on sync issues.

## Key screens

### Home / Marketing Dashboard (landing page)
- **Layout:** Widget grid similar to modern SaaS dashboards. Top strip: account alerts, quota usage (contacts, sends).
- **Primary widgets:** Recent campaign performance (last sent campaign's open/click/revenue), audience growth chart, upcoming scheduled sends, AI Marketing Assistant recommendations.
- **Post-Feb-2026 Omnichannel Dashboard:** Unified view of email + SMS + automation + ecommerce events. Revenue attributed to Mailchimp big-number at top, channel breakdown bars, top journeys, top products.
- **Quick actions:** "Create campaign" / "Build journey" / "View reports" buttons.

### Reports tab (top-level)
- **Sub-tabs:** Email Campaigns, SMS Campaigns, Landing Pages, Social Posts, Popup Forms, Automations, Comparative Reports, E-commerce (if store connected).
- **Report library:** Table view of every sent campaign, sortable by date/revenue/open rate/click rate. Filter by audience, tag, campaign type, date range.
- **Per-campaign report:** Full page with hero tiles (open rate, click rate, CVR, revenue), engagement line chart, click map overlay, bounce/unsubscribe list, product activity list, order history, industry benchmark comparison.
- **Download/export:** CSV and PDF on every report.

### E-commerce report (when store connected)
- **Layout:** Hero tiles — Revenue attributed to Mailchimp, Avg revenue per recipient, Avg order revenue. Below: Top products (table), Customer lifetime value (tile or mini-chart), Purchase trends over time (line chart).
- **Filter by store** if multiple connected.
- **Per-contact profile:** Chronological list of orders with items/totals/order numbers. Caveat: "some ecommerce platforms add past-purchase data that didn't result from Mailchimp messages" — historical backfill is shown but clearly labeled.
- Screenshot ref: https://mailchimp.com/help/view-revenue-from-email-campaigns/

### Customer Journey report (automation analytics)
- **Layout:** Each journey (workflow) gets its own report page with the journey map visible at top.
- **Per-step metrics on each journey node:** sent, opens, clicks, conversions, revenue.
- **Funnel completion rate** for the journey as a whole (e.g., "65% of entries completed this journey").
- **Individual message stats** link out to the regular email report for each step.

### Comparative Reports (Premium only)
- **Layout:** Select up to 5 campaigns → matrix view.
- **Columns:** Each campaign. **Rows:** Each metric (opens, clicks, CVR, revenue, AOV, unsubscribe rate).
- **Highlighting:** Best performer per metric is auto-highlighted green.
- **Charts:** Bar chart per metric comparing campaigns side-by-side.
- **Export:** CSV.

### Audience Dashboard
- **Layout:** Tile grid showing subscriber breakdown (source, engagement, location, recent activity).
- **Demographic insights:** Age range (predicted), gender (predicted), location map.
- **Growth chart:** List size over time with net-new / unsubscribed breakdown.
- **Tag and segment browser:** Jump from audience overview to segment-level filters.

### Campaign comparison (Comparative Reports + industry benchmark)
- On any individual campaign report, a "vs industry" overlay shows your open rate and click rate against the benchmark for your industry — as text and as a small bar.
- Link to full benchmarks report at https://mailchimp.com/resources/email-marketing-benchmarks/.

### Product performance (within E-commerce)
- **Top-selling items table:** SKU, name, units sold via email, revenue.
- **Sales trends over time:** Line chart per top-product.
- **Under-performing flag:** Products with declining sales highlighted for promo/pricing adjustment.

### Landing page / Popup form reports
- Separate sub-tabs; track visits, conversion rate, time on page, traffic source.
- GA-integration-aware — pulls session data if connected.

## Attribution model
**Last-touch with customizable windows per channel.**

Defaults:
- **Email:** 5 days from open, **30 days from click** (note: click window is 30 days — longer than Klaviyo's 5 days).
- **SMS:** 12 hours from delivery, 5 days from click.

**Custom windows** editable per Shopify integration (Manage Mailchimp for Shopify → Attribution settings → edit window).

**Revenue reported as net** — excludes taxes and shipping. Klaviyo includes taxes/shipping by default. This is a real gotcha when reconciling.

**Order matching:** When a store (Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Square) is connected, Mailchimp ingests orders via store webhooks + its own Site Tracking Pixel (Feb 2026). Order-ID direct match to contact profile.

**Known over-attribution paths:**
1. **30-day click window is aggressive** — a click 29 days before purchase credits Mailchimp even if multiple other touches happened in between.
2. Like Klaviyo, MPP auto-opens count unless bot filtering is enabled.
3. Contact-profile past purchases are backfilled from the store, which can look like attribution but isn't — help docs warn but UI doesn't always.

**Mailchimp vs Shopify reconciliation:** Mailchimp docs acknowledge that "some discrepancy is normal and expected" and guide that "Mailchimp should capture 40-70% of identifiable email-driven orders" — the same ballpark as Klaviyo. Direct quote: "the difference you may see is largely due to how attribution works."

**No native multi-touch attribution.** Only last-touch. Competitors position linear/positional/data-driven attribution as upgrades.

## Notable UI patterns worth stealing
- **Comparative Reports matrix view** — select N campaigns, get a metrics × campaigns grid with best-value auto-highlighted. Cleanest pattern in the category for "which of these did better." Nexstage's campaign comparison UX could lift this wholesale.
- **Industry benchmark overlay on every campaign report.** Not a separate screen — just a small "your value vs industry" badge on the relevant metric. Low-friction context.
- **Per-step metrics on the Customer Journey map** with funnel-completion % at the top. Conceptually similar to Klaviyo but the explicit "65% completed this journey" overall number is a clean summary Klaviyo lacks.
- **Net-revenue default** (taxes/shipping excluded) is more honest than Klaviyo's gross. Nexstage should show both clearly labeled rather than picking a side silently.
- **Bot filtering toggle on every report** with immediate visual re-render of filtered numbers. Train users that raw vs filtered are both legitimate views.
- **Per-contact order history on profiles** with "didn't come from Mailchimp" clearly flagged. Honest attribution UI — doesn't claim credit for backfilled data.
- **Multi-store filter on E-commerce reports** — merchant with multiple Shopify stores can scope any report. Table-stakes for Nexstage's workspace model.
- **AI Marketing Assistant recommendations on the dashboard** surfaced as individual cards ("Try sending on Thursday at 2pm, based on your audience patterns"). Actionable, not just "an AI chat tab."
- **Reduced-capability free tier that honestly surfaces what's gated.** Every paid feature on a free-tier dashboard shows an upgrade icon with hover text — doesn't hide the feature, doesn't let users waste time. Good pattern for Nexstage's free/paid split.
- **Audience Dashboard demographic tiles** (predicted age/gender/location) are a decent anchor for customer-analytics surfaces even when the predictions are imperfect.

## What to avoid
- **Don't charge for unsubscribed contacts.** Top-three complaint about Mailchimp for a decade and they still do it. Nexstage's per-contact or per-row pricing should default to billable = active.
- **Don't shrink the free tier every year.** Mailchimp's 2,000→500→250 slide has made "Mailchimp will downgrade you" a meme in the category. A stable free tier is a retention and trust asset.
- **Don't paywall basic ecommerce automation (cart abandonment, welcome series, post-purchase) at a premium tier.** Mailchimp's Standard gate for Customer Journey is the #1 "I'll just use Omnisend/Klaviyo free" reason.
- **Don't let store syncs silently fail.** Users don't notice until their revenue numbers look wrong — and then trust is gone. Sync-health UI should be visible and proactive.
- **Don't ship a generalist tool that claims ecommerce-first without the depth.** Mailchimp's pivot is undermined by Klaviyo/Omnisend having better product analytics, CLV, RFM, and segmentation. Pick a lane and execute deeply.
- **Don't bury attribution settings in a Shopify-integration subpage.** They're a reporting-control, not an admin-setting. Put them where the reports live.
- **Don't default to a 30-day click window without flagging it.** This makes Mailchimp's numbers look 20%+ higher than they probably should. Honest defaults > flattering defaults.
- **Don't cross-sell a parent company's ecosystem (QuickBooks, Intuit Assist) from inside the product.** Users coming for email marketing read this as ad creep.
- **Don't let support quality slide as you scale.** Mailchimp's post-IPO support reputation is a moat for competitors.
