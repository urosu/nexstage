# Hyros

**URL:** https://hyros.com
**Target customer:** Info-product sellers, course/coaching businesses, SaaS companies tracking trial-to-paid, high-ticket call-based sales funnels, performance agencies. Ecommerce is supported but is *not* the flagship use case — their "info marketer who books sales calls" persona is why the product exists. Scaling paid-traffic buyers spending $20k–$1M+/mo on Meta/Google are the bread-and-butter. Acquired by Banzai International (NASDAQ: BNZI) in Dec 2022 for $110M, rebranded as part of a broader martech suite.
**Pricing:** Revenue-banded, annual-contract-biased, **demo-gated** — you cannot see pricing or sign up without booking a call. No free trial, no self-serve.
- **Paid Traffic (monthly, based on tracked monthly revenue):**
  - $0–40k: **$379/mo** (annual $3,799 = ~$316/mo)
  - $40k–83k: $649/mo
  - $83k–250k: $1,089/mo
  - $250k–750k: $1,639/mo
  - $750k–1.25M: $2,199/mo
  - $1.25M–1.6M: $2,699/mo
  - $1.6M–2.08M: $3,299/mo
  - Unlimited: $4,399/mo
- **Hyros' own pricing page claims "starts at $230/mo" — this is the deeply-discounted annual rate, not monthly.**
- Agency and SaaS tiers exist as separate SKUs; pricing unpublished.
- AIR Agent (AI remarketing) is a separate add-on, also demo-gated.
**Positioning one-liner:** "Tracking proven to increase AD ROI for the most complex attribution challenges" — we find 250% more conversions than your pixel, and you can't argue because the numbers are "truth".

## What they do well
- **Aggressive, confident marketing.** Founder Alex Becker built his audience as a "loud-mouth marketer" (his own framing) — YouTube shorts, bold claims ("we catch everything your pixel misses"), Twitter dunks on competitors. The positioning is divisive but it *works* — Hyros is the default attribution tool in info-marketer Facebook groups and on sales-call-heavy agency rosters.
- **Call / lead-based attribution is genuinely strong.** Tying a booked sales call → phone number → ad click is the Hyros sweet spot. Integrations with Calendly, Acuity, call-tracking platforms, and phone-dialer CRMs are deeper than any ecommerce-first tool (Triple Whale, Wicked Reports, Northbeam).
- **Multi-touch LTV for subscription / SaaS.** Predicts long-term revenue, ARR, MRR, and churn from day one, so ad platforms can be trained on predicted-LTV custom conversions instead of day-0 signups.
- **AIR Agent** — AI-driven visitor identity resolution that tries to recognize returning anonymous users across devices/sessions and stitch them back to the original ad click. Positioned as the iOS-14 / cookie-deprecation workaround.
- **"ZERO changes to your ads. Set up in seconds"** is the install pitch — drop a script, connect ad accounts, Hyros takes over. Users report this is largely true for the happy path.
- **Drag-and-drop dashboard builder.** Most reviewers agree the dashboard *looks* polished — color-coded tiles, clean fonts, real-time trend graphs. Better visual polish than Wicked Reports.
- **1-to-1 dedicated analyst** included in every plan. A named human who will build custom reports, debug tracking, and run quarterly reviews. This is the "white-glove" pitch that justifies the price.
- **Unlimited custom reports, full API access, no feature gating.** Unlike Triple Whale's tiered feature set, Hyros' single plan includes every feature — you just pay more as revenue grows.

## Weaknesses / common complaints
- **"The attribution tool everyone loves to hate" is the real category perception.** Reddit, Trustpilot, and agency blogs feature a steady stream of teardowns. The complaints cluster around five themes below.
- **Pricing is opaque and feels adversarial.** You cannot get a quote without a demo call optimized to close you. The "$230/mo starting" marketing copy is a loss-leader annual rate; real monthly starts at $379 and scales to $4,399/mo. Multiple SegMetrics / Cometly comparison posts call this out explicitly.
- **Cancellation friction is a recurring Trustpilot complaint.** Representative quotes: *"tried to cancel, got told I was outside the 30-day window"*, *"when you try to cancel they make it really difficult and keep charging you"*, *"referred me to their ToS and said no"*. Multiple reviewers describe paying for months after attempting to cancel. This is the single most damaging pattern in the review corpus.
- **Over-reporting / lead inflation.** Trustpilot review: *"after 2 years, it only worked for the first 6 months, then started over-reporting leads data"*. Hyros' counterfactual math (claiming 250% more conversions than the pixel) can cross into double-counting, especially when CRM and CRO flows have their own deduplication.
- **Info-product vibe hurts ecommerce evaluation.** The product shows its roots — Alex Becker's marketing, info-product-heavy case studies, sales-call-optimized funnels. Shopify brands evaluating Hyros vs. Triple Whale / Wicked Reports frequently say it "feels like an internet-marketer tool" and pick a competitor.
- **Steep learning curve.** Once past the drag-and-drop dashboard, custom reports are "rigid" — reviewers who need specific views find they can't build them without the analyst. The "simple" surface masks a complex underlying data model.
- **Customer support is bimodal.** Dedicated-analyst users report great service; self-serve edge-case users report days of delay. Linx Digital (pro-Hyros agency) says Hyros support is better than Wicked's; Trustpilot's long tail disagrees.
- **Acquisition by Banzai has muddied the roadmap.** Post-$110M acquisition (Dec 2022), integration into the Banzai suite has been gradual; the product feels like it's between roadmaps in 2025/2026, and Alex Becker's personal brand no longer drives product decisions.
- **Ecommerce coverage is shallower than marketing suggests.** Shopify native integration exists; WooCommerce is second-class; Amazon and TikTok Shop support trails Triple Whale and Rockerbox. Creative analytics are far thinner than Triple Whale's Creative Cockpit.

## Key screens

### Main dashboard (drag-and-drop builder)
- **Layout:** Tile grid of widgets, drag-droppable, with a left sidebar for navigation. Color-coded tiles (green = above target, red = below) with simple sparklines.
- **Default widgets:** Revenue, ROAS, CPA, Ad Spend, Click Volume, Impressions, CTR, CPC, Sales, organized by traffic source. Campaign/source rows below the summary tiles.
- **Real-time refresh** — heavily marketed as "live" data (unlike many competitors' 15-minute cadence).
- **Sleek visual polish** is the most-praised UX element across reviews.
- **Screenshot refs:** https://app.hyros.com/dashboard (gated), Hyros YouTube channel walkthroughs, https://hyros.com/

### Attribution / source reports
- **Per-source breakdown** showing Hyros-attributed sales vs. platform-reported sales, per ad platform. The "discrepancy" is the sales pitch — Hyros claims 25–250% more conversions than Meta's native pixel reports.
- **Touchpoint-level journey:** for each sale, a chronological list of every tracked touch (ad click → email open → site visit → call booking → purchase), with timestamps.
- **Call/lead attribution:** phone numbers and booked calls appear as first-class touchpoints, with source-attributed breakdowns.
- **UTM + click-ID-level drill:** users can drill to a single click and see the full subsequent journey.

### Customer journey / LTV view
- **Cohort LTV** by acquisition source, with predicted-LTV curves extrapolated forward.
- **SaaS-specific:** ARR, MRR, churn projections per acquisition channel — unique among ecommerce-adjacent tools.
- **Subscription revenue attribution** — recurring payments automatically tied back to the original acquisition touchpoint.

### AIR Agent (AI remarketing)
- Visitor identity resolution dashboard — anonymous sessions matched to known contacts.
- **Match rate** as a KPI per platform (Meta, Google).
- Server-side events automatically fired to Meta CAPI / Google Enhanced Conversions with the enriched identity payload.

### Creative analysis
- Per-ad revenue, CPA, ROAS table. Thinner than Triple Whale's Creative Cockpit — no thumb-stop ratio, no gallery view, no cross-platform creative clustering. Described by reviewers as "functional, not a standout feature".

### Reporting / custom reports
- **Unlimited custom reports** via a builder. "Rigid" in reviewer language — powerful for supported use cases, frustrating when you need something non-standard.
- Scheduled email delivery and Slack integration.

## Attribution model
Hyros' public stance is that **there is one truth** and they find it. They do not expose a model selector in the Triple Whale sense; the user does not choose "first-click vs. last-click vs. linear". Under the hood Hyros runs a proprietary first-party pixel + AI-driven identity resolution (AIR) + UTM/click-ID matching, and presents a single "Hyros-attributed" number as the answer.

**Philosophy:** singular "truth". Everything else is wrong. This is why they sell so aggressively against the pixel ("we catch the 250% of conversions your pixel misses"), and why the over-reporting complaints sting — if you're selling truth, the moment your numbers diverge from the CRM the narrative collapses.

**Technical pillars:**
- Server-side pixel with extended cookie lifetime (first-party domain).
- **AIR** — AI-based visitor identity matching across sessions and devices.
- UTM + click-ID (fbclid / gclid) capture for deterministic matching when available.
- **Long lookback windows** — weeks-to-months, configurable but defaulted generously to maximize credit capture.
- **Call + lead matching** as first-class signals, not just web events.

Contrast: Triple Whale offers 7 models explicitly; Rockerbox triangulates MTA + MMM + incrementality; Wicked Reports sells one model tuned by TOF/MOF/BOF tagging. Hyros sells "we are the truth" — the most confident, least-hedged posture in the category.

## Integrations
- **Ad platforms:** Meta (CAPI), Google Ads (Enhanced Conversions), TikTok, YouTube, Microsoft Ads, Pinterest, X. Native CAPI/server-side outbound everywhere possible.
- **Ecommerce:** Shopify (native app), WooCommerce, ClickFunnels, Kajabi, Teachable, ThriveCart, SamCart.
- **CRM / call booking:** HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, Keap, Calendly, Acuity, GoHighLevel, Close, Pipedrive, Zoom, dialers.
- **Email/SMS:** Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit, Mailchimp.
- **Payment processors:** Stripe, PayPal, ThriveCart, SamCart.
- **Call tracking:** CallRail, WhatConverts, proprietary Hyros call tracking.
- **Outbound:** Meta CAPI, Google Enhanced Conversions, TikTok Events API — the "train the algorithm with our data" loop.
- Full API access on all plans.

## Notable UI patterns worth stealing
- **Drag-and-drop dashboard builder as the default landing surface.** Triple Whale and Northbeam both have customizable dashboards, but Hyros makes the customization the *first* experience, not a post-onboarding feature. The permission-to-shape is empowering.
- **Real-time refresh sold as a marketing message.** "Live data" is a visceral trust signal. Nexstage's 15-minute cadence should similarly be surfaced explicitly.
- **1-to-1 dedicated analyst baked into the plan price.** Reframes "customer support" as "your analyst" — a big psychological upgrade at the price tier Hyros charges.
- **Predicted LTV per acquisition channel, not just realized LTV.** Shipping forecast curves next to historical cohorts is a good pattern for any subscription/DTC tool.
- **Call / booked-call as a first-class touchpoint type.** Even for pure-ecommerce Nexstage, this is worth considering for brands with concierge/consultation flows.
- **"250% more conversions than your pixel" as a hero claim** (regardless of whether it's always accurate). The framing weaponizes the disagreement between platforms and the tool — the same disagreement Nexstage surfaces, but Hyros monetizes it as a headline.

## What to avoid
- **Don't make cancellation a dark-pattern.** The single worst thing in Hyros' review corpus. Every positive feature is undone by the "I can't cancel" Trustpilot thread. Nexstage should go the opposite direction — a visible self-serve cancel button, no call required.
- **Don't gate pricing behind a demo call if you want SMB trust.** The "book a call to see pricing" pattern reads as adversarial to anyone who's bought SaaS after 2020. Publish the revenue-banded price table.
- **Don't over-claim "truth".** Claiming a singular attribution truth sets you up for the "your numbers don't match my Stripe dashboard" support tickets that killed Hyros' Trustpilot score. Nexstage's "six sources disagree, here's by how much" posture is *safer* because it's honest.
- **Don't let the founder's persona be the marketing.** Alex Becker's YouTube brand sold Hyros in 2019–2022 and now alienates ecommerce buyers in 2025–2026. Product voice should outlive the founder's personal brand.
- **Don't ship info-product polish to an ecommerce audience.** The UX, case studies, and positioning all read as "coach marketing" to Shopify/Woo founders. If you want DTC credibility, the design language and testimonials have to match.
- **Don't launch a shallow creative analytics module.** Hyros' creative view is perfunctory and reviewers notice. Either ship Creative Cockpit-class depth or don't ship creative analytics at all.
- **Don't require a dedicated analyst to extract value.** If the self-serve experience is only 40% of the product, 40% of users churn inside 90 days — exactly the Trustpilot pattern.
